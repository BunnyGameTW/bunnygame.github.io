Present Memories

【Introduction】

In this visual novel adventure game, you play as Vox himself as he explores his memories and leave no stone unturned for an unforgettable experience with his clan of the past.

We return to the world of Sengoku, Japan in this unique game filled with stunning visuals inspired by ukiyo-e art, well thought-out gameplay reminiscent of the famous Ace Attorney series, and a simple but captivating story with mystery, twists and many surprises.

【Thoughts】

Vtuber, the so-called virtual idols are basically similar to the common real-life idols nowadays, except that they use the image of a virtual character to carry out their activities, and the common contents include singing, chatting, playing games and so on.
This game is to celebrate the birthday of Vox Akuma, the English-speaking Vtuber of Nijisanji.

Vox’s voice is really super low and sexy, the first time I heard it, I was enchanted, he is also super cute sometimes and very serious at streaming, zero days off week, you dare to believe, go to support him!
Watching him playing this game seems to be dreaming, super excited and so emotional🤩
The plot of the game has added many of the storylines that happened during the streaming, and there are also exciting fights and emotional episodes that make you cry.
The team also made a special website, and the artist Roku also helped all the developers to draw the character image, look how cute I am!

It’s the first time I used renpy, a game engine specializing in visual novels, the super famous DokiDoki Literature Department was also made with this engine.
In addition to the text logic, I’m also responsible for the gallery and the almost functional drawing horse. Renpy syntax is very simple, but it can also make a lot of special effects with python, and there is basically no big problem on the programming.
On the contrary, I think the most difficult part is communicating with foreigners, in fact, this is the first time I work with foreign partners, I am so nervous that I can’t talk properly, in the end, I have to respond to their questions by typing, whenever I have a meeting, I feel like I am doing a large English listening test🫠
But everyone was super kind and kept telling me I was doing great, and every team was super efficient and happy, and I was super happy with the final product.
